The route that worked for me was moire patterns. First version of this completely flopped, I was resizing images down to ~768px before doing the FFT, and that quietly blurs away the fine pixel-grid detail. At that point accuracy was 84%. Once I caught that and started doing the FFT on a few 512x512 patches cropped from the full-resolution image instead of the resized one, accuracy became 96%. Some other features i used were how blown-out the highlights are, Laplacian sharpness, color/saturation stats, and a check for horizontal banding and just used a logistic regression model for the final prediction. 
Accuracy: 96% on 5-fold cross-validation over my 100 photos (50/50 split).
Latency: ~480ms per image on my laptop CPU. Most of that time is doing a few FFTs on the full-res patches, that's the one part I think I'd try to speed up.
Cost: runs fully on-device for now, so basically free
What I'd fix with more time: I'd want way more variety in the training data before trusting this on photos I haven't seen. I'd also want to push the cutoff threshold up a bit rather than leaving it at 0.5, basically I'd rather catch more screens at the cost of a few more false flags, and send anything borderline (like 0.3–0.7) to a human instead of auto-deciding.
